About

Computer Science Student at Wellesley College, MA

Currently a junior studying CS, I enjoy problem solving, and try to focus my efforts on building products that provide utility to people in their daily lives.

Outside of the tech world, I'm passionate about bringing education to girls, especially in developing communities. I have been involved with Girl Up since 2016, from refounding my high school chapter to being on the e-board at Wellesley College. I also have a strong interest in the financial services industry, and enjoy learning about its intersection with technology. In my free time, I like to read, explore cities, and eat copious amounts of sushi.

Experience

Research and Development

I have experience in full stack development and data analysis. I have also dabbled in blockchain, lower-level systems programming (think networking and OS), and Android application development. I was a research assistant at Cybersecurity at MIT Sloan School of Management in Fall 2019, where I focused on data analysis and composition of survey results. Currently, I am doing data analysis for the Wellesley College Computer Science Department on the Codder tool, which provides feedback on problem set answers and code design for CS111 (Introduction to Computer Programming & Problem-Solving) students. The findings will be used to redesign and enhance the curriculum for CS111 for semesters to come.
